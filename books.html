Books I recommend

Sandman
Neil Gaiman
Sandman is a graphic novel that convinces with a deep leveled story, which prompts your creativity to play along. It includes adaptions of characters from mythologies, sagas and legends and is still far away from being too esoteric. Different artists were drawing different chaptes as usual but each style amazes by itself.
Windsänger-Trilogie
William Nicholson
In a very compelling way Nicholson describes different state systems and illustrates their advantages and disadvantages along a fascinating story.
Der Steppenwolf
Hermann Hesse
A contemporary novel about the warning of the Second World War and the loneliness of people with a vision.
Harry Potter-Reihe
J.K. Rowling
Starting as a children book Harry Potter grows with every book to become a wonderful series that describes the danger of isolation, racism, hunger for power and war and the necessety of friendship to stand up against that.
Die Jahrhunderttrilogie
Ken Follett
A genealogic viewpoint of different families around the world and their connections in the time around the First, the Second and the Cold War. It describes the horryfing but also noble nature of humanity.
Das zweite Gedächtnis
Ken Follett
A well built tension climax with the topic of the space race in times of the Cold War.
Das Bildnis des Dorian Gray
Oscar Wilde
Dorian Gray describes very vivid the natur of human beings and their dark abysses.
Tokio
Mo Hayder
Tokio is a novel about the present and the past of Japan and its connection to the Second World War.
Alice im Wunderland
Lewis Carroll
A wonderful story about daydreamers and how creative people can see the world. It's about acceptance of the differences.
Tage des Aufstands
Mascha Alechina
A description of conditions in Russia at present and maybe a warning of losing freedom of speech.
